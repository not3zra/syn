# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Security reviewers and human approvers who read Trust Receipts and approve or deny escalated agent actions; compliance officers who need regulatory-tier traceability and audit history; on-call engineers who deploy and operate syn; and hackathon judges or demo viewers who watch the live demo and need the differentiator (session-pattern correlation) to be obvious at a glance.

## Product Purpose

syn is a governance checkpoint for AI agents. It sits between an agent and the tools it calls, scores every action against six deterministic risk factors, correlates individually-low-risk actions into dangerous sequences, and either approves, escalates to a human, or blocks each call with a full audit trail and a plain-English explanation. Success is a reviewer who can make a fully-informed decision in seconds and trust that no risky sequence slips through.

## Brand Personality

Vigilant, precise, composed. Confidence through restraint, not alarms. The interface reads like a calm security console: it tells you exactly what happened, why, and what to do next.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS indigo dashboards and the default purple/indigo "AI tool" look.
- Glassmorphism and frosted-glass decoration.
- Marketing buzzwords ("seamless", "empower", "unleash", "next-gen", "world-class").
- All-caps eyebrow kickers above every section.
- Gradient text and side-stripe accent borders.

## Design Principles

- Show, do not tell: every decision carries its exact deterministic trigger, not a vague score.
- Composure under load: the UI stays calm and legible even when an action is blocked or escalated.
- One voice, one accent: a single brand accent used sparingly; decision colors carry status semantics.
- Density with rhythm: information is grouped and spaced intentionally, never a uniform wall of cards.
- Honest copy: verb-plus-object labels, no jargon, no em dashes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA contrast (body text at least 4.5:1, large text at least 3:1, placeholders included). Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`: every animation has a crossfade or instant fallback. Keyboard-operable controls with visible focus states.
